Physical profile

Age: 54 cycles (27 years).

Height: 1,5 meters (4,9 feet).

Weight: 14,5 kilograms (30,8 pounds).

Wingspan: 1,75 meters (5,7 feet).

Species: Laetuli.

Sex: Male.

Pronouns: They/Them, although accepts strangers and clients using He/Him

Marital Status: Not romantically active.

Residence: As of now a nomad with no fixed residence.

 Hair: Dark blue, similar to purple, green strains are found hidden throughout the tufts

 Eyes: Being arthropods Laetuli have technically a variety of colours in their many ommatidium, however to the naked eye it’s colour seems to be a very dark Blue

Skin: Light lavender, with a desaturated cobalt abdomen accented by dark blue portions

Wing colour: Dark grey, a variety of patterns can be found, a majority of them green

Antennae: Cobalt green, with yellow roots, dark cobalt spots can be found periodically appearing.

Notable markings: Upon the forehead lie two yellow circles, which have two more light lavender circles within them, these markings being found within Laetuli born during the Bloom

Normal clothing: Feathered hats, a majority of them green in colour.

Psychological profile

A connoisseur of the arts Maple’s Bloom believes that artistic production is the greatest manner to reflect one’s soul, be them visual or auditory, they reflect the artists deepest desires and opinions, any and all disrespect to the arts is a disrespect to Bloom themselves. For what would be life worth living if not for the Avante Garde.

Bloom is extremely soft hearted -both physically and mentally- resulting in them being friendly and trusting, even to those that are newly met, for seemingly no particular reason, this openness -however- comes at the cost that strikes that force their resolve to be tested have a tendency to lead to downward spirals of irrationality, hopelessness, paranoia and general sadness.

Due to not only their upbringing in a the Laetuli free territories and experience with nobility they have come to generally disrespect the authority of the crown, believing the nobility to be; “A bunch of absolute snobs that want to make fun of everyone bellow them” following this, Bloom has come to adopt a philosophy of “do as you please as long as it hurts no one” which they uphold zealously, becoming quite disliked by those in high places within the Simulian administration of the Southern Forests for their constant disobedience of Simulian law and bureaucrats.

Although welcoming to humanoids -regardless of their less than stellar first impressions- Bloom is, in all truth, rather suspicious of them, however desperately desires to be proven wrong in regards to their suspicion and stereotypes taught to them in their time within Laetuli society and later proven in the baron’s court.

Items of note

~Tools and Curios~

The Hurdy Gurdy;

“An unusual stringed instrument capable of emitting a melody unique to itself, being a whole band’s worth in a single piece.”

Although not inherently magical the tunes it can play bring out the best in people, boosting morale and motivating those that hear it to pursue their goals, a true testament to the power of the arts.

Fine Lute;

“For times of action and excitement, the Lute is dedicated to the call to arms and raised banners of the battlefield.”

A Lute made of fine wood, one can only assume to be the work of a master in their craft, it’s tunes are capable of getting out the adrenaline of those who hear it, the excitement of the strings translating well to the body of a soldier midst a fight.

Sack of sacks;

“It’s more of a purse really, may look small, but trust me, you can fit so many more sacks in there”

A small sack, capable of fitting on the palm of one’s hands, reportedly stolen from “A man with a very pointy hat” the space within it is greatly larger than one would expect, having enough space to roughly fit one to two average adult human males within it, currently it seems to be mostly filled with a variety of flamboyant headgear and many, many, many broken musical instruments.

Tyrant’s FigerBone;

“Heh. The last laugh was mine after all.”

A fingerbone of unknown origin, a ring can be found in it, seemingly made of gold with a sapphire like gemstone adorned upon it, according to the owner it’s a rather comedic sight, but to most it is just macabre.

~Weaponry~

Ornate Dirk

“Pointy, is it not?”

A well decorated dirk dagger meant for thrusting, forged in wootz steel it stands at 20 centimeters (7.8 inches), the blade itself is edgeless but it’s point is extremely well sharpened.

Iron Sickel

“Fits extremely well in a Mon-Keigh’s throat”

A simple farmer’s tool now utilized for cutting more than just rye, it’s iron blade stretches to 30 centimeters (11.8 inches) and suffers from partial rusting, however it’s curvature is well defined and edge sharpened being able to cut through tall grass with ease.

~Biological Defences~

Bug bite

Originally developed to bite the bark off of trees the mandibles of the Laetuli are extremely strong, being able to lacerate the flesh of most animals, crack their scales and -when under favourable conditions- even partially dig in to bone, Laetuli rarely use their bite against opponents as it requires that they come close to their target, a move that can expose their fragile body to a most likely fatal attack.

Corrosive spit

(2 turn cooldown)

A biochemical defense mechanism developed to fend off predators, by a combination of chemical compounds present in their abdomen, the Laetuli can create a high velocity corrosive substance that is expelled from their mouths (of which can travel 3 meters or 9 feet at 3 feet a second or 1 meter per second), this substance mostly causes a first degree chemical burns and irritation of the affected area, however, when spit continuously in the same general area effects may worsen, leading to potential fourth degree burns and even the melting of wood & under the right circumstances certain metals.

Natural Charisma (OOC permissions required)

Amongst hunters and explorers of the deep southern forests rumours run rampant of encounters with Laetuli, one idea is always prevalent and ever common in these tales, the apparent “cuteness” and “charisma” of this species, generally speaking these arthropods tend to make those around them feel more comfortable and jolly with their presence, the Laetuli have come to embrace this with most of those (very few) that leave their homes taking up jobs as entertainers, psychologists and professional pillows huggers in Simul.

Post-Mortem Volatility

Not exactly a skill nor an ability but more precisely a consequence of the chemical compounds in their abdomen, after dying the careful balance that allows these to exist deteriorates, leading to the abdomen of the Laetuli in question to explode soon after death, causing the chemicals within to be shot in multiple directions (clearing an area of 2 meters, or 6,5 feet in the explosion in a second)

Limited flight

Laetuli are capable of a limited form of flight, being that the species is capable of maintaining a maximum height of 3 meters (9 feet) above ground and a speed of 54km/h (33.7 miles/h) for an extended period (3-4 hours of flight out of combat, just 5 turns during combat) when buzzing their wings, normally -however- Laetuli prefer to stay at the same height as the head of whoever they may be speaking to.

~Songs of the Avante Garde~

( Obs; those affected by these sound based abilities are those targeted by the song in question, unless the song has no subject, if that is the case then the effects are felt by all sapien creatures in a 3,5 meters radius (11 feet) to a lesser extent. All the song's effects last 5 turns each and if used more than once in a battle the duration is reset, the same song cannot be played twice in a roll and their effects are dependent on the listener’s interpretation of the lyrics and melody, all of them having two explicit effects, one positive and the other negative being that the first explained effect is the intended effects of the song and the second one being the potential drawback. Suffice it to say all of these require OOC permission as they affect the mind of the targeted listeners)

Battle ballad

(Requires a Lute or Violin to perform, effects amplified when utilizing Lute) Lose not your grip on the dreams of the past, you must fight just to keep them alive! An energetic epic dedicated to a great warrior, the ballad is a delicate balance between energetic ups and suspenseful downs, one’s interpretation of these waves of emotion may very well be the difference between victory and defeat. Of course, one may take these tales as a triumphant show of will, boosting their confidence and giving them the morale boost needed for a decisive strike, however… One could also see these as just that, tales, nothing more, nothing less, a nice composure of course, but certainly nothing more than a mere nuisance midst combat.

Memento Mori

(Requires a Lute or Hurdy Gurdy, both being equally effective) I care not. And so I am immortal, a calm and collected song, telling of the beauty of life and serenity of death, a statement to the marvel of the never ending cycle of birth and slaughter. To the ears of the sussetable many reactions may come of this acceptance of destiny and the cycle of life, some will come to understand and embrace this cycle, fearless of what may wait for them behind the curtains they would feel reinvigorated, their wounds almost painless now that the material world loses its significance in the face of their eternal soul. Not all will embrace lady death and the inevitabilities of life however, they will take this as a grim reminder of the consequences of their actions, becoming fearful of the possible outcomes of a battle, shaking and losing sight of themselves, questioning why they’d even be here, risking the only life they have.

Every Rose has it’s Thorns

(Requires a Lute or Hurdy Gurdy, being that the Hurdy Gurdy is most effective) Every night has its dawn, this song is dedicated to reminding the listener that there is yet hope even when times are desperate and that nothing will ever conquer their will. Those that heed to a bard’s advice feel as if a burden is lifted, their hopes in the future restored adrenalynn once more rushes through their body healing their wounds and restoring their energies. But who’d listen to a court jester for advice? And if you were to really think about it even a thorn could kill if well placed! And just as the thoughts and doubt start to set into the mind the pain in the cuts worsens, stinging like a thousand bees as the listener finds themselves purposely holding to the thorns.

Maddening Mockery

(Requires a Violin or Lute to perform, being more effective when utilizing a Violin)(cannot be played in the user’s first turn) Look at this fool, this imbecile! Playing the hero, are you?! A whole dramatic poem dedicated mocking one’s insecurities, being it their virility or combat prowess those that hear it tend to lose faith in their own ability, wavering and overthinking their plans in hopes of proving the bard wrong, now under a self imposed pressure they find it difficult to hit strikes consistently. But of course not all have such a fragile ego, or mayhaps their ego is even more fragile, as some find themselves not filled with self doubt, but with anger and rage, with a new goal in mind; to put this clown in their rightful place, their fists heavier and more motivated than ever to meet with the bard’s soft face.

Finale!

(Any instrument may be used for the finale as long as it was used during one of the previous performances) Maybe just cut all their throats... To be sure. A step forward, a swift strike, a pain akin to that of a spear through the heart comes down the spine, every presentation has an ending and this Finale shall be grand! Finale can only be used after performing one -or more- other songs, it’s effectiveness is determined on how many songs were performed prior to it, when utilized the singer gives one last solo as they lunge at their target, decisively impaling them with a dirk, and as the curtains of life close the artist retreats back stage (their position prior to lunging) a job most exquisite indeed! The affected party is left in a rather deplorable state, their puncture wound bleeding the sudden nature of this attack leaves the target dazed (if OOC permissions are granted stunfor 1 turn) as they attempt to process how a large butterfly just stabbed them.

Biography

Born in the uncharted depths of Pluvia -or as those of Simul know it, the Southern Forest- during the great Bloom, Maple’s was baptised by the very leaf of which their larvae feasted upon so long ago, it was a simple life that of a Laetuli, no crown ruled over them, no man to dictate their strata, for -much like despicable pirates- they took to the practice of conducting themselves via consensus, each and every cargo taken up for those proven worthy of such crown in the face of the consulate, Bloom always had a wish to work in the arts, inspired by the Ballards of the previous community bard, they sought to learn from the best, so one day they too may lighten the day of their fellow, taking to listening to performances of outsider bards in the nearby settlements Bloom started to slowly understand how a bard should act, of course, nowhere near the skill and prowess of a true Laetuli artist, but the Mon-keigh would suffice for now.

Following the death of the chief of culture of their community Maple’s entered their bid, showcasing a most unique ballard indeed, inspired by the “primal” culture of the Mon-keigh in combination with Laetuli harmonious tunes they created what is now a masterpiece within Laetuli circles, the results were swift, and so was elected the best bard in Pluvia. In their success Bloom began to further study Simulian culture, all they sought was to improve their abilities, but what began as mere hobby in hopes of betterment slowly developed into a deep appreciation and fascination, Laetuli society never had much of an interest in contacting the outside as they feared that if others knew of them, they would be conquered and purged, much like the wildlife around them, taking their chances Bloom consulted with the Consulate, “Shall i be granted the privilege of leaving on pilgrimage? To learn of the Mon-Keigh?” the consuls believed them to have gone mad, what were they thinking? To approach the Mon-Keight would certainly mean death! But the bard insisted, what had he to lose but their chains? After incessant debate and bickering the go ahead was given, and so departed Bloom in their quest to learn and improve the arts.

In their innocent search for artistic inspiration Maple’s Bloom came under the service of a local baron, marginalized by their non-humanoid and peculiar guttural accent, the baron was able to do as he pleased with the Laetuli, forcing them under indentured servitude for the following years, every day the same routine, a court jester, knives were thrown in their despicable games, deprived of food and sleep Bloom had just about had it, their resolve tested the young bard put a finale to this stage play, one fateful night their mandibles dug deep into the barons flesh, ripping off his finger as a prize Maple’s left the the keep in secrecy stealing what they could off of the courtiers, to evade capture they left Pluvia for good.

Not entirely broken by this experience Bloom continued their quest of self improvement and learning, but now with the stereotypes they knew validated by the baron and the court they now wander cautious of the Mon-Keigh around them, of course, not all of the Mon-Keigh were like this, right? Right?
